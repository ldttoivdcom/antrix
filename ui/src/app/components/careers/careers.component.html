Regulatory Affairs Specialist

A regulatory affairs person in the field of medical devices plays a crucial role in ensuring that medical devices meet the necessary regulatory requirements and can be legally marketed and sold. Their responsibilities are diverse and can vary depending on the specific organization and the nature of the medical devices being developed or manufactured. Here are some common roles and responsibilities of a medical device regulatory affairs professional:

1. Regulatory Compliance:

  • Monitor and stay up-to-date with relevant laws, regulations, and guidelines related to medical devices, both domestic and international.
  • Ensure that the organization's medical devices comply with all applicable regulatory requirements.

2. Product Registration and Licensing:

  • Prepare and submit regulatory submissions, such as 510(k) premarket notifications, Premarket Approval (PMA) applications, CE Marking applications, and other regulatory filings.
  • Coordinate with regulatory agencies (e.g., FDA, EMA, or other regulatory bodies) to obtain product approvals and maintain licenses.

3. Quality Management Systems (QMS):

  • Develop, implement, and maintain quality management systems, such as ISO 13485, to ensure the quality and safety of medical devices.
  • Perform internal audits and support external audits by regulatory authorities.

4. Labeling and Packaging:

  • Review and approve labeling, packaging, and marketing materials to ensure they comply with regulatory requirements and accurately represent the device.

5. Clinical Trials:

  • Assist in planning and conducting clinical trials for medical devices, ensuring compliance with regulatory standards.
  • Submit Investigational Device Exemption (IDE) applications when necessary.

6. Risk Management:

  • Implement risk management processes, such as ISO 14971, to assess and mitigate risks associated with medical devices.

7. Post-Market Surveillance:

  • Monitor and report adverse events and other post-market safety and efficacy data to regulatory agencies.
  • Maintain post-market surveillance systems and ensure compliance with post-market surveillance requirements.

8. Change Management:

  • Evaluate and document changes to existing medical devices or manufacturing processes to ensure regulatory compliance.
  • Determine if regulatory submissions are required for significant changes.

9. Regulatory Strategy:

  • Develop and implement regulatory strategies for product development and market entry.
  • Advise cross-functional teams on regulatory considerations and implications.

10. Training and Communication:

  • Educate and train company personnel on regulatory requirements and changes.
  • Communicate with regulatory authorities and respond to inquiries or requests for information.

The specific roles and responsibilities may vary depending on the organization, the type of medical devices, and the applicable regulatory framework. Regulatory affairs professionals need to work closely with cross-functional teams, including research and development, quality assurance, marketing, and legal departments, to ensure that medical devices are compliant with all relevant regulations and can be brought to market successfully. They play a critical role in navigating the complex and ever-evolving regulatory landscape for medical devices.

Education Requirements: MS in Science and 10+ years of experience in medical device and drug approval process. Excellent knowledge of ISO 13485, EU IVDR, EU MDR, MDSAP, EMEA, EU and FDA regulations.

Software Quality Assurance Engineer

A medical device software regulatory affairs and quality assurance (QA) professional plays a vital role in ensuring the compliance and safety of medical device software. The responsibilities of an individual in this role can be diverse, with a focus on regulatory affairs, quality management, and the unique considerations related to medical device software. Here are the typical roles and responsibilities of a medical device software regulatory affairs and QA person:

Medical Device Software Regulatory Affairs:

1. Regulatory Compliance:

  • Stay informed about the latest regulatory requirements and guidelines related to medical device software, such as FDA guidance documents, IEC 62304, and ISO 13485.
  • Ensure that the organization's medical device software complies with all relevant regulatory standards.

2. Regulatory Submissions:

  • Prepare and submit regulatory submissions, which may include premarket notifications (510(k)), Premarket Approval (PMA) applications, or other relevant filings, depending on the software's classification.
  • Collaborate with regulatory agencies to obtain clearances or approvals.

3. Risk Management:

  • Implement and maintain risk management processes for software in accordance with ISO 14971, identifying and mitigating potential risks associated with the software.

4. Labeling and Documentation:

  • Review and approve labeling, documentation, and user manuals to ensure they meet regulatory requirements and accurately represent the software's intended use and functionality.

5. Clinical Evidence:

  • Assist in planning and conducting clinical evaluations or studies for medical device software when necessary to demonstrate safety and efficacy.

6. Software Validation and Verification:

  • Ensure that software validation and verification processes are in place, adhering to relevant standards (e.g., IEC 62304), to demonstrate that the software functions as intended and is safe to use.

7. Change Management:

  • Evaluate and document changes to the software or its development processes, determining if regulatory submissions are required for significant changes.

Quality Assurance (QA):


1. Quality Management System (QMS):

  • Develop, implement, and maintain a QMS specific to medical device software, such as ISO 13485, to ensure the quality, safety, and efficacy of the software.

2. Software Development Processes:

  • Establish and oversee software development processes that align with regulatory requirements and quality standards, including design controls, software lifecycle processes, and configuration management.

3. Validation and Verification Testing:

  • Plan and execute thorough validation and verification testing to ensure the software meets quality and regulatory standards.

4. Document Control:

  • Manage document control procedures, ensuring the accurate and up-to-date documentation of all software development activities.

5. Audits and Inspections:

  • Prepare the organization for regulatory audits and inspections related to medical device software.
  • Address findings, implement corrective actions, and continuously improve quality and compliance.

6. Post-Market Surveillance:

  • Monitor and analyze post-market data, including adverse event reports, to identify and address potential safety or quality issues related to the software.

7. Training and Education:

  • Educate and train employees on regulatory compliance and quality assurance processes.

These roles and responsibilities are designed to ensure that medical device software is developed, maintained, and marketed in a manner that meets both regulatory requirements and the highest standards of quality and safety. Collaboration with cross-functional teams, including software development, regulatory affairs, quality assurance, and clinical experts, is essential to navigate the regulatory landscape and successfully bring medical device software to market.

Education Requirements:

MS in Science Field and 5+ Years of Experience. Thorough understanding of ISO 13485, EMEA, EU and FDA regulations.
